Organic house is a House subgenre that emerged in the late 2010s. It is mainly linked to Deep House and Downtempo, as it is similarly a slower, more introspective, relaxed and organic form of Electronic Dance Music. Progressive House was influential as well, as can be heard in organic house's delicate production and long, gradual song structures, sometimes sharing traits with Melodic House. While it usually has a tempo of around 120 BPM, it can be sometimes as slow as 100 BPM. The drums are subtle, attentive and aim for hypnotic rhythms, which are often influenced by Afro House. Nonetheless, organic house's instrumentation and samples are typically borrowed from West Asian Music and South Asian Music, instead of Southern African Music. The melodies often use or emulate acoustic instruments such as marimbas, violins, vibraphones, and various other traditional instruments. This further draws similarities to Tropical House, but the genre aims for a much more spiritual, mature atmosphere compared to the joyful sound of tropical house. Additionally, sounds like foley noises, vinyl crackles, and Field Recordings further help to embrace its natural character.


Organic house gradually arose from the 2010s deep house and Tech House/Minimal Techno scenes, with DJs like Sébastien Léger, Lee Burridge, and Oliver Koletzki developing the sound. That being said, many of the pioneers would be rather new artists such as Elfenberg, Volen Sentir and Roy Rosenfeld. Labels like All Day I Dream, Melody of the Soul, Sol Selectas, TRYBESof and Anjunadeep became the main hubs of the genre. Organic house has found particular popularity in desert-located festivals like Burning Man, as well as club scenes of Mediterranean resort spots. In the early 2020s, the genre established its sound and "organic house" as its name.
